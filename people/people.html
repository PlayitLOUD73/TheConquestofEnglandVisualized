People

Harold Godwinson, King of England

He succeeded to the throne after Edward the Confessor's death in 1066. On Edward's deathbed, Edward proclaimed Harold as his heir. Bennett wrote, "It was customary for a king to designate an heir on his deathbed, and Edward efectively had no choice. He died on 5 January 1066 and the next day Harold was crowned king..." (24). Tostig, Hardrada, and William all pursued their claims on the throne.

Harold led his army against the Norweigans and soundly defeated them with a suprised attack at the Battle of Stamford Bridge. Harold then marched South to engage William's army at Hastings. The battle started off well until Harold was struck in the eye with an arrow. The chaos that followed ended in Harold's death and the dispersion of the English army at Hastings (Bennett 43).

William, Duke of Normandy

William became the Duke of Normandy at the age of 7. In the year 1064, Harold swore an oath to William (the subject of the oath is debated). When Harold was crowned King of England, William was able to claim rights to the throne based on Harold's slight agains him. Freeman wrote in William the Conqueror, "... the wrong done by Harold to William gave William a casus belli against Harold, and that William, if victorious. might claim the crown of England... ...by right of conquest" (Chapter VI).

William invaded England and fought Harold in the Battle of Hastings, defeating the English and taking Harold's life. William became known as William the Conqueror, and he spent the rest of his days solidifying his rule over England (Chapter IX).

Harald Hardrada, King of Norway

Harald Hardrada (or the "the Hard Ruler") became King of Norway in 1047. He had a claim to the English throne and invaded Northern England. He and Tostig routed the English at Fulford. At the Battle of Stamford Bridge, Harald and his men were suprised by the English army. Manley wrote in "Last of the Vikings", "When Hardrada... took an arrow to the throat, the balance tipped in favor of the English" (Manley).

Tostig Godwinson, former Earl of Northumbria

Tostig was Harold's brother, and he laid his own claim to the English throne. He was expelled by Northumbria in 1065, and Morcar replaced him (Bennett 24). Tostig raided Northern England and later joined forces with King Harald. He was killed in the Battle of Stamford Bridge.

Edwin, Earl of Mercia and Morcar, Earl of Northumbria

Edwin and Morcar were brothers and joined the rebellion to overthrow Tostig from Northumbria. Morcar became the new Earl of Northumbria. They unsuccessfully led an English against Hardrada and had to retreat. After Harold's death, Morcar and Edwin led a resistance that was crushed by William. They tried to revolt again in 1068 and 1071 but failed both times (Cannon).
